What the updated data says

1. The old fixed-board CRT alias problem is gone from the decoder output: `master_origin_row` / `master_origin_col` now report the
   physical board placement directly.
2. The large axis-aligned board-frame jumps that previously split one target across 2–4 quadrants are fixed. On the fresh
   `130x130_puzzle` fixed-board run, `target_00` collapses into one shared board-frame region across all six snaps.
3. Adjacent-camera shared-ID overlap improved materially:
   - old fixed-board run: `4530` shared IDs across sequential camera pairs
   - new fixed-board run: `9400`
4. Per-target ring-neighbour consistency improved from `1 / 20` targets to `16 / 20`.

Current implication

The remaining failures are no longer consistent with a decoder-frame bug. They are concentrated in weak narrow views with low corner count.
When two snaps now share a decoded corner ID, they agree on `target_position` exactly.
